Revenge of the Nerds

by J. Brian Shumate
January 12 2015

Being a nerd in the 1980s was an arduous existence.

A great threat to swelling one’s brain with knowledge and cavorting with code and circuits, especially in a university setting was the stereotypical jock.

Brutish and banal, these beastly characters were to be avoided and feared for they could pummel a fragile pencil-necked geek into a pocket-protected pulp.

There were even films made to reflect this condition, with the most popular being “Revenge of the Nerds”.

In this movie, the nerds use their supreme intelligence to overcome thuggish football players in the ultimate celebration of brains over brawn.

Indeed, the nerddom evidenced in the 1980s movies slowly became a sort of cause célèbre that eventually elevated nerds to mythical status as the information age progressed.

Long gone are the once justified fears of feeble-minded ogres beating the shit out of poor nerds.

Nerds watch our every move, know our every desire and favorite thing, then use this information to try and sell that thing to us.

Nerds are hostile whether unintentionally or not — to other nerds not like themselves, and essentially impede the progress of humankind with exclusion and prejudice.

Nerds are at the heart of wickedly clever financial heists where the crooks never leave the comforts of their Aeron.

Nerds form the center of subterfuge and sabotage while waging war as state-sponsored threat actors.

Nerds help design an build robotic death machines which seek and destroy both intended targets along with so-called collateral damage.

Nerds skirt the law within three-letter government entities to soak up all of our data, store them, classify them, mine them, and use them against us.

Gone are the days of worrying about jocks — alas, all nerds (and the rest of the world) have to worry about now are other nerds.

Nerds have taken their revenge on the whole world.
